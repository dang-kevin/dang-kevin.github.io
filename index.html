Welcome!

My name is Kevin Dang and I graduated from the University of Toronto in Spring of 2020. I completed a specialist in applied statistics and a minor in mathematics.

Currently I am working at GlobalData Plc as a Medical Data Analyst in their Toronto office. Majority of my time is spent working on a pricing tool that shows insightful pricing information about medical device brands on a global scale. The markets that are addressed in this project include cardio (e.g. aortic stent grafts) and neurovascular (e.g. flow diverters). Software used for this project include Python (pandas) and Excel (pivot tables). In addition to my projects, I am also required to write press releases and articles, which are linked at the bottom of this page. Everyone who writes articles in medical devices are referred to as Medical Device Analysts, however my role is unique as I am a Medical Data Analyst.

Since June 2020, I have been working at Export Development Canada as an intern on the Business Performance Analytics (BPA) team and the Business Intelligence Centre (BIC) team under the Business Intelligence, Reporting and Analytics (BIRA) division. In September, I converted to part-time when I starting working full-time at GlobalData. My responsibilities include creating dashboards in PowerBI and Excel to provide succinct summaries of targets and results for SVPs, VPs, and other employees at EDC. I also produce weekly reports which involve querying data and running SAS EG projects and process flows.

Previously, I worked as a Data Analyst at the University of Toronto Centre for Industrial Relations and Human Resources. The project I worked on involved analyzing data on wages in the agriculture and farming industry, led by Dr. Greg Distelhorst. I also provided Statistical Consulting services for a management consulting firm to answer some questions they had regarding user data which they collected from their health improvement platform. Prior to these roles, I worked as a Research Assistant at the Rotman School of Management under the supervision of Dr. Chris Liu on projects about scientific publications and careers.
